Who we treat

You don't have to fit a template.

Most people who find us have already tried something. A clinic that discharged them too early. A protocol that didn't account for the rest of their body. We start from scratch every time: your body, your pattern, your goal.

Who comes here

  • You've done PT before. It helped a little, then plateaued. Or didn't help at all. 01
  • Your child has scoliosis. You were told to watch and wait. You'd like another option. 02
  • You're postpartum and something still isn't right. Pelvic floor, core, or just feeling like yourself again. 03
  • You had surgery and want to recover properly. Spine, shoulder, hip: hands-on rehab, not a handout. 04
  • You want to get back to sport, or running, or just moving without thinking about it. After injury, surgery, or a long time away. 05
  • You've been told there's nothing else to do. There usually is. 06

First visit

Ninety minutes. One therapist. No assumptions.

The first visit is an evaluation. Not a treatment session, though most people leave feeling different. Your therapist takes a full history: symptoms, timeline, what you've tried, what hasn't helped. They look at posture, breath, gait, and load patterns across the whole body before narrowing in on your specific complaint. Ninety minutes of actual attention.

By the end, you'll have a real assessment and a proposed plan: number of sessions, frequency, and what progress looks like for you specifically. You'll leave with one or two things to work on. The plan is a starting point. Your body refines it every visit.
