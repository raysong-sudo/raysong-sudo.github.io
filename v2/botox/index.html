What we offer

No upselling. No packages. What you need, placed precisely.

Dr. Park uses Botox for two categories of treatment: therapeutic and aesthetic. Therapeutic Botox targets the masseter muscle to reduce jaw clenching, the temporalis for tension headaches, and the mentalis for chin dimpling related to jaw dysfunction. Aesthetic Botox addresses frown lines (glabella), forehead lines, and crow's feet, placed conservatively to maintain natural expression.

Dermal fillers are used for volume restoration, nasolabial folds, marionette lines, and cheek definition. Dr. Park draws on his maxillofacial anatomy training, which means the placement is precise and the doses are conservative. There's no quota to fill, no add-on services to recommend. The consultation is one thing: figuring out what's right for your face.

Questions

  • Is this a medspa?

    No. This is a clinical practice with a DDS on staff who holds the training and licensure for both dental work and medical aesthetic treatments. The environment is clinical, the approach is conservative, and the goal is accurate placement, not volume sales.

  • How long does Botox last for jaw tension?

    For therapeutic jaw applications (masseter, temporalis), Botox typically lasts 3 to 6 months. The effect on grinding and headache frequency is usually noticeable within 2 weeks of injection. Many patients repeat every 4 to 5 months.

  • Do I need to come in for a consultation first?

    For fillers, yes, a consultation is always the first step. For Botox, the consultation and treatment can often happen in the same appointment if the area and dose are clear. Book a consultation and we'll determine the right approach.

  • Will I look overdone?

    Not the goal. Dr. Park's aesthetic philosophy is conservative, enough to make a visible difference, not so much that the result reads as injected. If you've had experiences with overdone results elsewhere, describe what you didn't like. The consultation is a conversation, not a sales pitch.
